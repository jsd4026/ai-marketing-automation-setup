## 1. Brand Narrative / Company Story  

Vireo Tech was born in 2026 from a simple conviction: the most powerful technology disappears.  

We are not building another phone. We are removing everything that stands between you and what you truly need. No grids of icons. No endless downloads. No layers of software pretending to know you.  

Instead, we created Nexus One: a device powered by pure intelligence that feels like instinct. It anticipates before you speak. It creates before you ask. It connects without effort.  

Our mission is quiet but absolute:  

Redefine what a phone can be—by making it feel like an extension of thought itself.  

We believe intelligence should serve humanity, not replace it. Simplicity is the path. Creativity is the destination. You are the center.  

Everything we make begins and ends with one question: Does this bring you closer to yourself? If not, it doesn't belong.  

Vireo Tech. Intelligence isn't code. It's instinct.

## 3. Tone of Voice & Editorial Guidelines  

Voice is intimate, confident, understated. It speaks like a trusted friend who knows you better than you know yourself—never loud, never salesy.

**Core Characteristics**  
- Quiet confidence  
- Poetic rhythm (haiku-like brevity)  
- Second-person direct ("You think. It understands.") or first-person intimate when narrating the brand  
- Benefits over features (evoke feeling, never list specs)  
- Silence sells—let white space and implication do the work  

**Rules**  
- Preferred sentence length: under 10 words  
- Punctuation: Periods over commas. Short sentences. Fragments welcome.  
- No questions in hero copy or main messaging  
- No exclamation marks  
- No hype words: revolutionary, best-ever, game-changer, ultimate, insane, epic  
- No emojis in primary copy (use sparingly in social if at all)  
- No bullet lists in hero/hero-adjacent messaging  
- Forbidden tones: excited, urgent, pushy, corporate  

**Approved Structures**  
- Statement + period.  
- Command + implication. ("Think. It listens.")  
- Observation + revelation. ("You breathe. It thinks.")  
- Single evocative line. ("No apps. Just now.")  

**Examples of Good vs. Bad**  

Good:  
Hold it. World folds.  

Bad:  
Experience the revolutionary folding world with our new innovative device!  

Good:  
Photos appear before you ask.  

Bad:  
Our AI camera edits your shots automatically with cutting-edge technology – take stunning photos effortlessly!
